## Working with other software engineers

Many of the achievements made by humans would not have been possible without collaboration. My experiences with working with other people have been mostly developed in the academic environment. I hope to expand the scope of my teamwork experiences by working alongside other like-minded individuals in the field of software engineering. The idea of coming together with other people to collaborate, innovate and solve problems is truly exciting to me.

## Challenge accepted

Who doesn’t love a good challenge? Solving difficult problems might be one of the top things that come to mind when I think about my future in software engineering. Concepts such as abstraction and modularization are essential to solving really big problems. I can’t help but get that oh so satisfied feeling when a well thought out plan is executed, and the goals are accomplished. 


## Software enables us

I currently work as information technology intern. At work, I am always reminded that most of the products of software engineering are for the purpose of enabling people to be more productive. Software is just a means to an end. Lines of code are intrinsically meaningless to a person; It’s the utility that software can bring us that is valuable. I’d like to have a well-rounded understanding software. I would like this understanding to include everything from the idea, all the way to the implementation. In the future, I hope to extend my skillset towards implementation.

## Bringing it all together

Hackathons include a combination of everything above. These software-engineering events often require people to collaborate in attempting to solve a difficult problem through a solution that can be readily implemented. To date, I’ve participated in just one hackathon – the Hawaii Annual Code Challenge. I hope to regularly participate in various hackathons to augment my software-engineering chops.
