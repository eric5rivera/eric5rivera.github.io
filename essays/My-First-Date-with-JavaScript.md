I sat before my computer screen, fingers laid stiff and warm across the home row as I took a deep breath before my plunge into my first session of JavaScript. I was excited, anxious and worried all at the same time. Although JavaScript isn’t my first programming language, that “Hello World” in JavaScript felt just as underwhelmingly exhilarating as the first time.

From the blogs, tutorials and books that I have skimming about the subject, I found that JavaScript can be used in both front and back end development. This versatility is what makes the language an appealing one. It almost seems like a multi-tool that is ready to be deployed in various situations. Working through the Coding Camp exercises was a pleasant experience as the various features, syntax and structure was highlighted. I have only made a dent in the practice problems available, and I am excited to work through more so as to build up my chops. 

Compared to Java, C, and C++, I have found that the underlying concepts in programming remain the same. So far, it has been the nuances and quirks of the language that stand out the most to me. I often find myself remembering last split second, “oh that’s right, I don’t need to specify the data type”. The most challenging part of learning this language thus far has been getting used to implementing callback functions. I found the exercises in the ES6 section require the most focus to internalize the concepts.

Taking a step back, I see that this new language is only a tool in the practice of athletic software engineering. Through the Workouts Of the Day (WOD), I have been able to put my newly gained skill to the test. Working under pressure can be stressful, but the experience was pleasurable in the payoff. Whether it’s a live in-class WOD, or a practice WOD, the hard-technical skills of myself are put into overdrive to do my best. It’s the challenge and sense of growth that sustains interest in a new endeavor.

I will work to learn more about JavaScript and its use in software engineering. The environment of a software engineering class is an excellent kickstart for the development of the useful skills and tools required in today’s technology driven world. I am confident that the skills gained through Workouts Of the Day and JavaScript are bound to pay dividends for my career in information computer science.
